From which course can students look for part-time work?

It is better for students to start looking for part-time work in their 1st and 2nd year.
In the final years, studying is no more difficult, but it will seem that the workload has become higher.
Moreover, the approach of the graduation project will make students nervous. What are your thoughts on a part-time job? If you manage to find an excellent vacancy during the 2nd and 3rd courses, then in a couple of months
you will be able to adapt to the new educational and work regime.
After six months, it will become quite easy for you to combine all your
responsibilities and even writing a thesis will not affect your part-time job.

Ways of making money as a student

Part-Time Jobs

Look for part-time jobs that fit your schedule. Many places hire students for evening or weekend shifts.

Internships

Gain practical experience in your field of study through internships. Some internships are paid, and even unpaid ones can provide valuable experience.

Content Creation

If you enjoy creating content, consider starting a blog, YouTube channel, or a podcast. Once you have a following, you can monetize through ads, sponsorships, or merchandise.

Freelancing

If you have skills in writing, graphic design, programming, or other areas, consider freelancing.

Tutoring

If you excel in a particular subject, offer tutoring services to fellow students or even high school students.

Photography

If you're good at photography, offer your services for events, portraits, or stock photo websites.
